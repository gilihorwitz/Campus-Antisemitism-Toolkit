Email Templates

Ready-to-use email templates for contacting administration, faculty, and other relevant parties. Customize these templates with your specific details.

Template 1: Initial Report to Dean of Students

Subject: Report of Antisemitic Incident on Campus Dear [Dean's Name], I am writing to report an antisemitic incident that occurred on [DATE] at [LOCATION]. As a Jewish student at [UNIVERSITY NAME], I believe it is important to bring this to your attention. INCIDENT DETAILS: [Brief description of what happened - 2-3 sentences] IMPACT: This incident has [describe impact - e.g., "made me feel unsafe on campus," "affected my ability to focus in class," "created a hostile environment"]. I have documented this incident and can provide additional details, evidence, or witness statements if needed. I would appreciate the opportunity to discuss this matter further and understand what steps the university will take to address this situation. I am available to meet at your convenience. Please let me know how you would like to proceed. Thank you for your attention to this matter. Best regards, [YOUR NAME] [YOUR EMAIL] [YOUR PHONE NUMBER] [STUDENT ID NUMBER]
✓ Copied!

Template 3: Email to Professor (Classroom Incident)

Subject: Concern About Antisemitic Comments in [COURSE NAME] Dear Professor [NAME], I am writing to bring to your attention an incident that occurred during [CLASS DATE/TIME] in [COURSE NAME]. During [class discussion/lecture/group work], [brief description of what happened]. As a Jewish student, I found these comments [describe impact - e.g., "hurtful," "inappropriate," "creating a hostile learning environment"]. I would appreciate the opportunity to discuss this with you, either during office hours or at another convenient time. I am also happy to provide suggestions for how we might address this in a way that promotes understanding and respect. Thank you for your attention to this matter. Best regards, [YOUR NAME] [YOUR EMAIL]

Email Best Practices

  • Be professional but firm: Maintain a respectful tone while clearly stating your concerns.
  • Be specific: Include dates, times, locations, and names when possible.
  • State the impact: Explain how the incident affected you and your sense of safety.
  • Request action: Ask for a meeting, follow-up, or specific response.
  • Keep records: Save copies of all emails and responses.
  • CC appropriately: Include relevant administrators, but don't spam everyone.
  • Follow up: If you don't hear back in 3-5 business days, send a polite follow-up.

This toolkit was created by a UNC student who experienced campus antisemitism firsthand. It's a work in progress—if you have feedback or suggestions, please reach out.
