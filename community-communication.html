Community Communication Template

When incidents happen, your community needs to know. This template helps you communicate clearly, share resources, and maintain community support without causing unnecessary panic.

Community Update Template

SUBJECT: Update on Campus Antisemitism Incident Dear [COMMUNITY NAME - e.g., "Hillel Community," "Jewish Students," "Friends"], I'm writing to share an update about [an incident that occurred / ongoing concerns about campus climate]. WHAT HAPPENED: [Brief, factual summary - 2-3 sentences. Include date, location, and basic facts without unnecessary detail that could retraumatize.] WHAT WE'RE DOING: - [Action 1 - e.g., "We have reported this to the Dean of Students"] - [Action 2 - e.g., "We are meeting with administration on [DATE]"] - [Action 3 - e.g., "We have documented the incident thoroughly"] RESOURCES AVAILABLE: - Report incidents: ReportCampusHate.org - Legal support: ADL Legal Support (www.adl.org/contact) - Campus resources: [List relevant campus offices] - Mental health support: [List resources] HOW YOU CAN HELP: - [Specific action if applicable - e.g., "If you witnessed this incident, please reach out"] - [Support needed - e.g., "We need community members to attend the meeting on [DATE]"] - [General support - e.g., "Check in on your Jewish friends and classmates"] NEXT STEPS: [What happens next - e.g., "We will provide another update after our meeting with administration on [DATE]"] IMPORTANT REMINDERS: - Your safety is the priority. If you feel unsafe, don't hesitate to contact campus security. - Document any incidents you experience or witness. - You are not alone. Reach out if you need support. If you have questions or concerns, please reach out to [CONTACT INFO]. We will get through this together. [YOUR NAME] [YOUR ROLE/TITLE IF APPLICABLE]

Communication Guidelines

What to Include

  • Facts: What happened, when, where (without graphic details)
  • Actions taken: What you've done, what's being done
  • Resources: Where people can get help
  • Next steps: What happens next, when to expect updates
  • Support: How people can help or get help

What to Avoid

  • Graphic details: Don't retraumatize people with unnecessary specifics
  • Speculation: Stick to facts, not assumptions about motives
  • Panic language: Be clear about seriousness without causing unnecessary fear
  • Personal attacks: Focus on incidents and responses, not individuals
  • Premature conclusions: Don't promise outcomes you can't guarantee

Different Communication Channels

Email/Group Chat

Best for: Detailed updates, formal communication, reaching many people at once

  • Use clear subject lines
  • Keep it organized with headers
  • Include action items clearly
  • Provide contact info for questions

Social Media

Best for: Quick updates, resource sharing, raising awareness

  • Keep it brief and clear
  • Link to more detailed information
  • Be mindful of privacy and safety
  • Consider who can see your posts

In-Person/Meetings

Best for: Sensitive information, community support, discussion

  • Create a safe space for sharing
  • Have resources available
  • Allow time for questions
  • Follow up in writing with key points

Timing Considerations

  • Immediate: Share urgent safety information right away
  • After reporting: Update community after you've taken initial action
  • After meetings: Share outcomes and next steps
  • Ongoing: Regular updates keep community informed and engaged

Protecting Privacy

  • Don't share others' personal information without permission
  • Be careful about identifying witnesses or other affected students
  • Consider using BCC for sensitive emails
  • Respect that some people may not want to be publicly associated

When to Seek Help

If you're unsure about what to communicate or how, consider reaching out to:

  • Hillel or Chabad staff
  • ADL or other advocacy organizations
  • University communications office (for official statements)
  • Trusted faculty or administrators

This toolkit was created by a UNC student who experienced campus antisemitism firsthand. It's a work in progress—if you have feedback or suggestions, please reach out.

This site complements (not competes with) official resources like ADL and Hillel. Use what's helpful, adapt what you need.
