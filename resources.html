Resources

This toolkit complements official resources from established organizations. Here are key resources for reporting, support, advocacy, and mental health.

Reporting & Legal Support

  • ReportCampusHate.org

    Anonymous reporting platform for campus antisemitism incidents. Reports are shared with relevant organizations and can help track patterns.

  • ADL Legal Support

    Legal resources and support for Jewish students facing antisemitism on campus. Contact ADL for legal advice, support, and help with Title VI complaints.

  • Department of Education Office for Civil Rights

    File Title VI complaints for discrimination based on shared ancestry or ethnic characteristics. Jewish students are protected under Title VI.

Advocacy Organizations

  • ADL (Anti-Defamation League)

    Reports on antisemitism, provides educational resources, advocates for policy changes, and can engage directly with universities on behalf of students.

  • Hillel International

    Jewish student organization with chapters on most campuses. Provides community, support, and can advocate for Jewish students with university leadership.

  • StandWithUs

    Educational organization that provides resources, training, and support for students facing antisemitism and anti-Israel bias on campus.

  • AMCHA Initiative

    Monitors and documents antisemitism on college campuses, provides resources, and advocates for Jewish students.

  • Jewish on Campus

    Student-led organization that amplifies Jewish student voices, documents incidents, and advocates for change on campuses.

Mental Health & Support

  • Jewish on Campus Mental Health Resources

    Curated list of mental health resources specifically for Jewish students dealing with campus antisemitism.

  • Crisis Text Line

    Free 24/7 crisis support via text. Text HOME to 741741. Available for anyone in crisis, including those dealing with discrimination and harassment.

  • 988 Suicide & Crisis Lifeline

    Free, confidential 24/7 support for people in distress. Call or text 988. Available for anyone experiencing emotional distress.

  • Your Campus Counseling Center

    Most universities have free or low-cost counseling services. Check your university's website for information. Many offer crisis support and ongoing therapy.

  • Hillel/Chabad Staff

    Your local Hillel or Chabad staff are often trained to support students and can provide emotional support, resources, and connections to professional help.

Educational Resources

  • ADL: Understanding Antisemitism

    Educational resources about antisemitism, its forms, and how to respond. Useful for understanding what you're experiencing and educating others.

  • StandWithUs Education

    Educational materials, training programs, and resources for students and educators about antisemitism and Israel-related issues.

Campus Resources

Your university likely has offices that can help. Look for:

  • Dean of Students Office: Handles student conduct, support, and can address incidents
  • Title IX Office: Handles discrimination complaints (though Title VI may be more relevant for antisemitism)
  • Diversity & Inclusion Office: May have resources and can advocate for inclusive campus climate
  • Campus Security/Police: For immediate safety concerns and to report criminal activity
  • Counseling Center: Mental health support
  • Student Government: Can advocate for policy changes and student concerns

How This Toolkit Complements These Resources

This toolkit is designed to work alongside (not replace) these official resources:

  • Official organizations provide advocacy, legal support, and institutional power
  • This toolkit provides practical, student-created templates and frameworks
  • Use both: Report to official organizations AND use these templates for your own documentation and communication
  • Share feedback: If you find gaps or have suggestions, let both official organizations and this toolkit know

Important Notes

  • These resources are current as of when this toolkit was created, but organizations may update their websites or services
  • If a link doesn't work, try searching for the organization name directly
  • Many of these organizations have local chapters or representatives—reach out to see what's available in your area
  • Don't hesitate to contact multiple resources—you don't have to choose just one
  • If you're in immediate danger, call 911 or campus security

This toolkit was created by a UNC student who experienced campus antisemitism firsthand. It's a work in progress—if you have feedback or suggestions, please reach out.

This site complements (not competes with) official resources like ADL and Hillel. Use what's helpful, adapt what you need.
